Summary: At Microsoft Research's AI Frontiers, Luciano is developing the next-generation products using the latest large language models (LLMs) technology. As a senior research engineer, he focuses on creating highly efficient LLMs through distillation and adaptive computation, with the goal of broadening LLMs use through making them more cost-effective.

During his time at Goldman Sachs, Luciano leveraged AI-based technology to develop and implement innovative financial applications, improving processes and generating new internal products. His work focused on the transformative potential of LLMs in processing complex data and generating insights.

Luciano's academic background includes a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Max Planck for Informatics and a master's in Computer Science from Imperial College London. Additionally, he holds an advanced bachelor's degree in Economics from the University of Buenos Aires and a master's in Economics from the University of San Andres. He also co-founded Ambiverse, a spin-off of the Max Planck Institute, where he continued exploring the use of language technology for various industrial applications.

Luciano's diverse background provides him with a broad perspective that allows him to approach challenges from multiple angles in the search of innovative and effective solutions to drive innovation.
